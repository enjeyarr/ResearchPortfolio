I'm Nathan, a design researcher. I consider myself a qualitative researcher focused on products and services. I also have experience doing data work and statistics for non-profits and can flex into UX and front-end as well.

My time as a user researcher at the agency BEAM Interactive jumpstarted my industry research journey. Before that, I did mainly ethnography from my anthropology background, including studying abroad in the Yucatan, Mexico.

I recently graduated from Carnegie Mellon's Masters of Human-Computer Interaction program. I focused on applied design research including areas of VR, social impact, and games research. I just published a paper with Professor Jessica Hammer on meaningful games (viewable in a few weeks!)

I'm currently seeking full-time researcher positions in the Bay Area. Feel free to reach out about anything!

In my free time, I enjoy tabletop gaming, drag shows, and getting better at cooking. I've recently been interested in design/research for social impact. I'm excited to have already started engaging with meetups and talks with designers and anthropologists in the Bay Area around these topics.
